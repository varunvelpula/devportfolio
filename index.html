Experience

ModLab, University of Pennsylvania

Graduate Research Assistant

I am working on the ModQuad project under the guidance of Prof. Mark Yim. The problem statement is to develop a decentralized control algorithm for multi-quadcopter systems which work together in a collaborative swarm. The aim is to develop a decentralized control algorithm for the self-assembly of multi-quadcopter systems during mid-flight.

To achieve modularity, the initial stages of the project I had developed multiple moular stucture designs (cages in which the quadcopter sit in) and prototyped them through 3D prinitng and assembly of the structure.

Autonomous Vehicle Lab, Indian Institute of Science (IISc)

Research Assistant

This is my summer internship in the Department of Aerospace Engineering, Indian Institute of Science under the guidance of Prof. Ashwini Ratnoo. Here, I developed the controls and implemented tracking and guidance laws for UAVs.

I developed an experimental setup consisting of autonomous Micro Aerial Vehicles (MAVs) to test laws of guidance such as Pure Pursuit and Good Helmsman and tracking laws based on potential vector fields.

I implemented the controls using PID tuners for navigation of MAVs in indoor environment using data from IMU sensor and array of motion capture cameras. I had further implemented sensor fusion using Kalman filter in order to minimize noise and improve data reliability.

I had developed the network for communication of data among MAVs, motion capture system and workstation using Robot Operating System (ROS) and Matlab.

ABB Robotics

Intern

This was my summer internship after my sophomore year.
Here, I worked on the integration of an anthropomorphic robotic arm gripper with an ABB industrial robot IRB 1600 to explore its capabilities as a personal robot. Potential applications like typing, writing and pick place operations of irregular shaped objects were explored through experiments.

I had designed a gripper based on the open source design by sculptor and designer, Gael Langevin. This design incorporated the under actuation principle to achieve the human-like finger movements.

The gripper was interfaced with the robot using an Arduino microcontroller and ABB robot controller IRC-5 to control the gripper finger movements which were using servo motors.

Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) using GraphSLAM

GraphSLAM is used to generate and constantly update the environment around the robot in real time and localize itself in this environment. I implemented a cost effective solution by using an ultrasonic sensor for distance measurement and camera for obstacle detection instead of a stereo camera or a LIDAR sensor. The algorithm developed is based on GraphSLAM as described in the Udacity course on Artificial Intelligence in Robotics by Dr. Sebastian Thrun.

Positive Drive Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

I had done this project in my sophomore year. I developed a novel design of a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) incorporating positive drive. The CVT is an automatic transmission based on belt drive driven between two pulleys at different diameters. In CVT, the diameters can be adjusted dynamically to alter the speed ratio. In our implementation, positive drive is achieved using a retractable gear tooth when engaged with the steel belt. The aim of the design was to improve fuel economy, increase torque transmission and improve driving experience for truck drivers.
